## Provident Fund (PF) {#provident-fund}

### What is Provident Fund (PF) and is it mandatory in India?

Yes. The Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) is mandatory for all establishments with 20 or more employees, and for employees earning up to INR 15,000/month in basic + DA (Dearness Allowance). Once a company is covered, contributions apply to all employees regardless of salary if they opt in.

### What percentage does the employer contribute to PF?

The employer contributes **12% of the employee's basic salary + DA** each month. This is split as:
- **3.67%** → Employee Provident Fund (EPF) corpus
- **8.33%** → Employee Pension Scheme (EPS) — capped at INR 15,000 basic
- **0.50%** → EDLI (Employees' Deposit Linked Insurance)
- **0.50%** → EPF administrative charges

The **employee also contributes 12%** of basic + DA, going entirely into the EPF corpus.

### What does the employee contribute to PF?

The employee contributes 12% of (Basic + DA) per month. This is deducted from the employee's gross salary. Employees earning more than INR 15,000/month in basic may voluntarily contribute on the full amount, but the employer's obligation is capped at 12% of INR 15,000 = INR 1,800/month for EPS.

### Can an employee with a salary above INR 15,000 opt out of PF?

New employees joining after earning more than INR 15,000/month as basic may opt out of EPF at the time of joining, but only if they have never been EPF members before. Existing EPF members cannot opt out.

### What is a UAN and why does it matter?

The Universal Account Number (UAN) is a 12-digit number assigned to every EPF member by EPFO. It is portable across jobs and must be seeded with Aadhaar and PAN. Foreign companies hiring in India must ensure every employee has an active, KYC-linked UAN before the first contribution. Failure to do so results in rejected contributions.

### How is PF calculated under the new Labour Codes (2025-26)?

Under the Labour Code on Wages 2019 (effective 2025-26), **Basic + DA must be at least 50% of the total CTC (Cost to Company)**. This rule significantly increases PF contributions compared to the old practice of keeping basic salary low. For example, if an employee's CTC is INR 1,00,000/month, the basic must be at least INR 50,000, making the employer PF contribution INR 6,000/month (12% × 50,000) instead of the older typical INR 2,400/month.

## Employee State Insurance (ESI) {#employee-state-insurance}

### What is ESI and who must contribute?

The Employees' State Insurance (ESI) scheme provides medical, disability, maternity, and unemployment benefits. It is mandatory for establishments with 10 or more employees where any employee earns up to INR 21,000/month gross salary (INR 25,000/month for employees with disabilities).

### What are the ESI contribution rates?

| Contributor | Rate    | Basis                  |
|-------------|---------|------------------------|
| Employer    | 3.25%   | Gross wages            |
| Employee    | 0.75%   | Gross wages            |
| **Total**   | **4.00%** | **Gross wages**      |

Employees earning ≤ INR 137/day (approx. INR 3,000/month) are exempt from the employee portion; the employer still contributes 3.25%.

### What benefits does ESI provide to employees?

ESI-covered employees and their dependants receive:
- Free medical treatment at ESIC hospitals and dispensaries (no cash limit)
- Sickness benefit: 70% of wages for up to 91 days/year for certified illness
- Maternity benefit: 100% of wages for 26 weeks (for insured women)
- Disability benefit: 90% of wages for temporary/permanent disablement
- Dependant's benefit in case of work-related death

### If an employee's salary exceeds INR 21,000 after a raise, do we stop ESI?

If an employee's wage exceeds INR 21,000 mid-contribution period, ESI contributions continue until the end of that contribution period (6 months). ESI operates on two contribution periods: April–September and October–March. The employee exits ESI at the start of the next period.

## Gratuity {#gratuity}

### What is gratuity and when is it payable?

Gratuity is a statutory lump-sum benefit paid by the employer to an employee upon exit — whether due to resignation, retirement, or death/disability. Under the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972 (subsumed into the Labour Code on Social Security 2020), gratuity becomes payable after **5 years of continuous service** (except in cases of death or disability, where there is no minimum tenure).

### How is gratuity calculated?

```
Gratuity = (Last drawn basic + DA) × 15/26 × Number of completed years of service
```

- **15/26** represents 15 days' wages for every 26 working days in a month
- Years exceeding 6 months round up to the next full year (e.g., 5 years 7 months = 6 years)
- Tax-free up to INR 20,00,000 for private sector employees

**Example:** An employee with INR 60,000/month basic + DA and 7 years 8 months of service:
- Completed years = 8 (because 7 years 8 months rounds up)
- Gratuity = 60,000 × (15/26) × 8 = INR 2,76,923

### What is the gratuity provisioning rate for payroll planning?

As a planning estimate, employers provision **4.81% of basic + DA** per year for gratuity liability. This is an actuarial approximation and does not replace formal actuarial valuation for accounting purposes (AS 15 / Ind AS 19).

### Is gratuity paid immediately upon resignation?

The employer must pay gratuity within 30 days of it becoming payable. Delayed payment attracts simple interest at the rate notified by the government (typically 10% per annum). An employee can apply for gratuity using Form I under the Gratuity Act.

## Professional Tax {#professional-tax}

### What is Professional Tax (PT) and who levies it?

Professional Tax is a state-level tax levied on salaried individuals and self-employed professionals. It is administered by state governments and municipalities — not the central government. Not all states levy PT; major states that do include Maharashtra, Karnataka, West Bengal, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana.

### What is the maximum Professional Tax in India?

The Constitution caps Professional Tax at **INR 2,500 per year** (INR 200–250/month depending on the state slab). In Maharashtra, the maximum is INR 2,500/year for employees earning above INR 10,001/month.

### Who is responsible for deducting and remitting Professional Tax?

The employer is responsible for deducting PT from the employee's salary and remitting it to the relevant state authority. Employers must register under the applicable state PT Act and file periodic returns.

## Leave Entitlements {#leave-entitlements}

### What are the statutory leave entitlements under India's Labour Codes?

The Labour Code on Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions (OSH Code) 2020 harmonizes leave rules. Key entitlements:

| Leave Type | Minimum Entitlement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Earned Leave (EL) / Annual Leave | 1 day per 20 days worked | After 1 year of service; carry-forward capped at 30 days |
| Sick Leave (SL) | As per State rules (typically 7–12 days/year) | Many employers offer 12 days |
| Casual Leave (CL) | As per State rules (typically 7–12 days/year) | Not carried forward in most states |
| Maternity Leave | 26 weeks (first 2 children); 12 weeks (3rd+ child) | Maternity Benefit Act 1961 |
| Paternity Leave | No statutory minimum | Many employers offer 5–15 days |
| National/Festival Holidays | 3 national holidays (compulsory) + state-notified holidays | Republic Day, Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanti |

### Can earned leave be encashed?

Yes. Under the Labour Code, employees can encash earned leave in excess of 30 days at the time of separation or, in some states, during service as well. Leave encashment at separation is tax-exempt up to INR 3,00,000 for private sector employees.

### What is the maternity leave entitlement for remote employees?

Remote employees are entitled to the same maternity leave protections as on-site employees under the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act 2017. Employers with 50+ employees must also provide crèche facilities or an allowance equivalent.

## Bonus {#bonus}

### Is a performance bonus mandatory in India?

The **statutory bonus** under the Payment of Bonus Act 1965 (now Labour Code on Wages 2019) is mandatory for establishments with 20+ employees. It applies to employees whose basic + DA is ≤ INR 21,000/month.

### How is statutory bonus calculated?

- **Minimum bonus**: 8.33% of annual basic salary or INR 100 (whichever is higher)
- **Maximum bonus**: 20% of annual basic salary (if the employer has allocable surplus)
- **Calculation ceiling**: Capped at INR 7,000/month or the minimum wage (whichever is higher)
- Payable within 8 months of the close of the financial year (i.e., by November 30 for April–March FY)

Employees are eligible after completing 30 working days in an accounting year.

### What is a performance-linked variable pay (PLVP) and is it regulated?

PLVP is a discretionary component above the statutory minimum. It is governed by the employment contract and is not covered by the Bonus Act. However, if PLVP is consistently paid and forms part of the employee's reasonable expectation, courts have in some cases treated it as a contractual entitlement. Clear contract language is essential.

## Health Insurance (Private / Group Mediclaim) {#health-insurance}

### Is private health insurance mandatory for employees in India?

There is no central statutory obligation for private employers to provide health insurance beyond ESI. However, post-COVID, several states and industry regulators have issued advisories, and SEBI requires listed companies to provide group health insurance. Best-in-class employers typically offer:

| Cover Type | Typical Sum Insured | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee only | INR 3–5 lakh | Minimum market standard |
| Employee + spouse + 2 children | INR 5–10 lakh | Most common package |
| Employee + parents | INR 5–10 lakh (top-up) | Premium add-on; increasingly common |
| Top-up / super top-up | INR 10–25 lakh | Cost-effective for higher cover |

### What does a group health insurance policy typically cover in India?

Standard group mediclaim policies cover: hospitalization (in-patient), pre- and post-hospitalization (60 and 90 days), daycare procedures, maternity (with waiting periods or riders), and AYUSH treatment. Mental health coverage is now mandatory under IRDAI guidelines following the Mental Healthcare Act 2017.

### How is group health insurance priced in India?

Group health insurance premiums depend on group size, age profile, claims history (for renewals), and sum insured. Indicative premiums for a group of 10–50 employees:
- INR 5,000–8,000/employee/year for INR 3 lakh cover
- INR 10,000–15,000/employee/year for INR 5 lakh cover including parents

Premiums paid by the employer are fully tax-deductible as a business expense.

## National Pension System (NPS) {#national-pension-system}

### What is NPS and is employer contribution mandatory?

The National Pension System (NPS) is a voluntary, market-linked pension scheme regulated by PFRDA. For **central government employees**, NPS is mandatory (Tier-1). For private sector employees, it is **optional** but tax-advantaged.

### What is the tax benefit of employer NPS contribution?

Under Section 80CCD(2) of the Income Tax Act, employer contributions to NPS up to **10% of basic + DA** are tax-deductible for the employee (no INR limit, above the INR 1.5 lakh Section 80C cap). Under the new tax regime (FY 2025-26), this deduction is **14% of basic + DA** — making NPS a highly tax-efficient benefit.

## Other Supplementary Benefits {#other-supplementary-benefits}

### What are common supplementary benefits offered by Indian employers?

| Benefit | Typical Value | Tax Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| House Rent Allowance (HRA) | 40–50% of basic (non-metro/metro) | Partially exempt under Sec 10(13A) |
| Leave Travel Allowance (LTA) | 1 month's basic × 2 trips in 4 years | Exempt with travel proof |
| Meal vouchers (Sodexo/Zeta) | Up to INR 2,200/month | Fully exempt |
| Mobile/Internet reimbursement | INR 1,000–2,500/month | Exempt with bills |
| Car lease (company car) | As per grade | Perquisite tax applies |
| Stock options (ESOPs) | Varies | Taxed at exercise (as perquisite) and sale |
| Flexi benefit plan (FBP) | 15–25% of CTC | Mix of exempt and taxable |

### What are flex benefit plans (FBP) and how do they work?

FBPs allow employees to allocate a portion of their CTC across multiple tax-friendly reimbursement heads (meal vouchers, fuel, professional development, etc.). This reduces the employee's taxable income. FBPs are popular in mid-to-large companies but require payroll system support to administer.

## Fully Loaded Cost Calculation {#fully-loaded-cost-calculation}

### How do I calculate the true employer cost for an India-based employee?

The fully loaded monthly employer cost is approximately **1.2081× the employee's monthly CTC**.

```
Fully Loaded Cost = CTC × 1.2081

Breakdown (as % of CTC):
  CTC itself:                100.00%
  Employer PF (12% of 50% basic):   6.00%  [12% × 50% CTC]
  Employer ESI (3.25% of gross):    3.25%  [if applicable, on full gross]
  Gratuity provision (4.81%):       4.81%  [4.81% of basic, approx 2.40% of CTC]
  EDLI + admin charges (0.65%):     0.33%
  Group health insurance:           ~1.00% [estimate, varies]
  Other admin / payroll processing: ~1.00%
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  Approximate total:               ~116–121% of CTC
```

> **Note:** Use 1.2081× as a conservative planning estimate. Actual costs vary based on salary structure, ESI applicability, and benefit choices. For precise numbers, use the [Versatile Club Salary Calculator](https://versatile.club) or consult an EOR provider.

## EOR vs. Own Entity for Benefits Administration {#eor-vs-own-entity}

### Should we use an EOR or set up our own entity to manage India benefits?

For companies hiring 1–25 employees in India, an **Employer of Record (EOR)** is almost always more cost-effective than a wholly owned subsidiary. Key considerations:

| Factor | EOR | Own Entity |
|---|---|---|
| Setup time | 1–2 weeks | 3–6 months |
| Compliance overhead | Managed by EOR | Full internal burden |
| PF/ESI registration | Handled by EOR | Must register separately |
| Benefits customization | Limited to EOR's plan | Full flexibility |
| Cost | 8–15% markup on salary | Fixed overhead + compliance costs |
| Scale threshold | Best for < 25 employees | Worthwhile above ~25–50 employees |

### What statutory filings must an employer make for benefits in India?

Monthly filings include: ECR (Electronic Challan-cum-Return) for PF by the 15th of each month, ESI challan by the 15th of each month, Professional Tax challan (frequency varies by state), and TDS (Form 24Q) quarterly. Annual filings include the PF annual return, ESI annual return, Form 16 issuance to employees, and actuarial valuation for gratuity (for entities under AS 15).
