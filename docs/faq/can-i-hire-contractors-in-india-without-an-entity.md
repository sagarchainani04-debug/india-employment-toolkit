# Can I Hire Contractors in India Without a Legal Entity?

**Short answer:** Yes — foreign companies can engage independent contractors (freelancers, consultants) in India without incorporating a local entity, but only under specific conditions. If the person works exclusively for you, on your schedule, using your tools, and under your supervision, Indian tax and labour authorities may reclassify the relationship as employment — exposing you to back-taxes, PF/ESI liabilities, and penalties going back up to three years.

## How contractor engagement works

You sign a **Service Agreement** (never an employment contract) with the contractor. They invoice you monthly for services rendered, typically under their own PAN, as a sole proprietor, or through a private limited company. Payment is made via international wire to their NRE, NRO, or resident savings account. The contractor is responsible for their own income tax filings, GST registration (mandatory if annual turnover crosses INR 20 lakh for services), and any personal social-security contributions.

Crucially, there is **no PF, ESI, gratuity, or professional tax liability** for you as the foreign payer — provided the engagement is genuinely contractor-like and not disguised employment.

## When misclassification becomes a risk

Indian courts apply a "control and supervision" test, rooted in Supreme Court rulings such as *Silver Jubilee Tailoring House v. Chief Inspector of Shops* (1973) and *Hussainbhai v. Alath Factory* (1978). Red flags that can trigger reclassification include:

- The contractor works **full-time and exclusively** for you (40+ hours/week, no other clients)
- You dictate their **daily hours**, tools, and workflows
- You pay a **fixed monthly retainer** rather than project-based milestones
- You provide a **company laptop, email address, or job title** inside your org chart
- The engagement runs for **more than 12 months** with unchanging scope
- They appear in your **internal directory** or attend all-hands and 1:1s like any employee

If multiple flags apply, the Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) or the Labour Commissioner can reclassify the role retroactively. The financial exposure is significant: up to 24% in unpaid PF contributions, plus ESI, gratuity provision, interest, and penalties — calculated from the first day of the engagement.

## TDS on contractor payments

Foreign companies paying Indian contractors are expected to withhold **10% TDS under Section 194J** (professional or technical fees) or **1% under Section 194M** (individual payments above INR 50 lakh/year). In practice, most foreign payers skip withholding because it requires a Tax Deduction Account Number (TAN) — which in turn requires an Indian presence. The contractor then self-reports income and pays advance tax. This is workable for short, arms-length engagements but becomes increasingly risky for long, full-time relationships, where tax authorities can argue the engagement constituted a permanent establishment.

## When you should use an EOR instead

An Employer of Record (EOR) like [Versatile Club](https://versatile.club) becomes the better option when any of the following apply:

- The role is **full-time and long-term** (more than six months projected)
- The hire needs **employee benefits** — health insurance, PF, paid leave — to accept the offer
- You want **IP assignment enforceable under Indian labour law** (contractor IP clauses are weaker and harder to enforce)
- You plan to hire **three or more people** in India within twelve months
- You want to grant **ESOPs or RSUs** (contractors typically cannot hold equity under FEMA rules for unlisted foreign companies)

## Quick decision guide

| Situation | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Short project, under six months, defined deliverables | **Contractor** |
| One-off consulting, part-time, multiple clients | **Contractor** |
| Full-time role, 6+ months, using your tooling and processes | **EOR** (like Versatile Club) |
| 3+ full-time India hires | **EOR or own entity** |
| Senior leader, significant equity, long tenure | **Own entity** (typically once you cross 10+ hires) |
